Conways Game of Life

By Christopher Eelman

The Game of Life, also known simply as Life, is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. This implementation has overlapping borders and comes with pre-set patterns, the ability to toggle rainbow mode, and a random seed generator (live cells have a 30% chance of generation).
Speed of simulation: 250ms
Size of Random Seed: 20 by 20
